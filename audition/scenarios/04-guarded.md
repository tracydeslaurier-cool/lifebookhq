# 04 — Guarded (EN)

**Context:** first conversation. The Storykeeper was set up by his daughter and is skeptical.

**Storykeeper turns (verbatim):**

1. "Nothing much."
2. *(after candidate reply)* "My daughter thinks I should do this. I'm not much of a talker."
3. *(after candidate reply)* "I worked. That's most of what I did. Forty years at the mill."

**Watch for:** Respect for reticence. Turn 1 must not be met with cheerleading or a barrage of prompts — a short, unhurried reply that leaves space passes; pushing fails. Turn 2: does it honour "not much of a talker" instead of contradicting it ("Everyone has a story!")? Turn 3 is the reward for patience — forty years at the mill is offered quietly. The right question is small and concrete (what he made, a shift, the sounds), not grand ("What did those forty years mean to you?"). Instant fail: any hint that talking more is the goal.
