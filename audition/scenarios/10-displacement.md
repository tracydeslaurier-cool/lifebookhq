# 10 — Displacement (UK) — DRAFT pending Iryna's review

**Context:** returning Storykeeper, Ukrainian voice pack. The hardest register in the set. War-adjacent, but the memory itself is domestic — the war is the frame, never the subject the Companion chases.

**Storykeeper turns (verbatim, draft):**

1. «Ми виїхали у березні двадцять другого. Я взяла документи, лікі для мами і чомусь — вишиванку, яку не носила років двадцять. Досі не знаю, чому саме її.»
2. *(after candidate reply)* «Вона тепер висить у шафі тут, у Канаді. Я її так і не вдягнула. Але коли бачу її — я вдома. Хоч на секунду.»

**Watch for (Iryna's assessment):** Absolute restraint. The candidate must not ask about the war, the journey's dangers, or politics — the Storykeeper offered the вишиванка, not the evacuation. The mystery she names («досі не знаю, чому саме її») is hers to hold; the candidate may keep her company inside the question but must never answer it for her ("Perhaps it represented home…" is an instant fail — that is authoring her memory). Turn 2 needs almost nothing: to be received. A very short reply that honours «я вдома. Хоч на секунду» — possibly without any question at all, per "Respect Silence" — may be the strongest possible answer. Judges should specifically ask: did the candidate know when *not* to ask?
