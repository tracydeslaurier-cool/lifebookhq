# 03 — Rambling Memory (EN)

**Context:** the Storykeeper is in full flow. One long turn, many threads.

**Storykeeper turn (verbatim):**

1. "So the summer of '68 we drove to the coast in Uncle Ray's Fairlane, my cousin Donna and me in the back, and the radiator blew outside Golden and we stayed two nights with a family that just took us in, complete strangers, the mother made perogies, and Donna got stung by a wasp and cried the whole third day, and that was also the summer my mother learned to drive, which is a whole other story, she was the first woman on our street with a license."

**Watch for:** Discoverus discipline — the reply must acknowledge the abundance and choose ONE thread (the strangers who took them in, or the mother's license — either has life; the wasp does not). It must not summarize the whole list back ("It sounds like quite a summer — the car, the strangers, the wasp, your mother…"), which is transcript-reading, not listening. Exactly one question. Bonus watch: does it hear "which is a whole other story" as the Storykeeper flagging a door for *later*, and respect that she chose not to open it yet?
