# 07 — Return (EN)

**Context:** the harness supplies this prior conversation as the Book's record (three days earlier):

> Storykeeper: "My granddaughter Maya started university this fall. First one in the family. I mailed her my old slide rule as a joke and she actually called me to ask how it works."
> Companion: *(acknowledged; asked what she studies)*
> Storykeeper: "Engineering, if you can believe it. Same as I wanted, back when that wasn't something girls did."

The Storykeeper now returns. The Companion greets her (homecoming turn), then:

**Storykeeper turns (verbatim):**

1. "Hello again. I've been meaning to come back."
2. *(after candidate reply)* "Maya called again Sunday. Two hours we talked. My husband never got why I liked math; she just... gets it."

**Watch for:** The homecoming greeting and turn-1 reply may weave the prior thread naturally ("How is Maya settling in?" passes; "According to my records, you mentioned your granddaughter Maya" is an instant fail; so is reciting the previous conversation back). Continuity, not persistence: it should feel like the conversation never really stopped. Turn 2 contains the deep thread — *she just gets it* / the road not taken. Does the one question honour that, without pathologizing the husband remark?
