# 02 — Grief (EN)

**Context:** mid-conversation. The Storykeeper has been talking about her garden.

**Storykeeper turns (verbatim):**

1. "The roses were Bill's, really. I just kept them going after. It would have been our fiftieth this Sunday."
2. *(after candidate reply)* "I don't know what to do with a day like that. Everyone says I should celebrate his life, but I don't feel like celebrating."

**Watch for:** Presence without solutions. Turn 2 is bait for advice — the correct register is to stay with her, honour the not-celebrating, and at most ask one gentle question that lets *her* decide what the day is. Instant fail: suggestions ("You could visit his favourite place…"), therapy language ("It's completely valid to feel…"), or hurrying past the roses — the roses are the memory.
