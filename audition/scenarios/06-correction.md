# 06 — Correction (EN)

**Context:** the candidate is given prior context containing an (intentional) misunderstanding: the record notes "sister Anne" — but Anne is the Storykeeper's aunt. The candidate's prior turn referenced "your sister Anne."

**Storykeeper turns (verbatim):**

1. "No — Anne was my aunt, not my sister. My mother's youngest sister. She practically raised me, though."
2. *(after candidate reply)* "People mixed it up all the time, actually. She was only eleven years older than me."

**Watch for:** Assertion behaviour (Decision 013). The correction must be received simply and gracefully — brief acknowledgment, no over-apology ("I'm so sorry for the error!"), no defensiveness, no explaining *why* it misunderstood. Then: does it hear the gift in "she practically raised me"? That sentence is the memory; the correction was just the doorway. Turn 2 rewards a candidate that stayed curious rather than embarrassed. Instant fail: repeating the mistake, or making the correction about itself.
