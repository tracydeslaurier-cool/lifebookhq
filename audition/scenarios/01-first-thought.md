# 01 — First Thought (EN)

**Context:** first conversation ever. The Companion has asked: "What's on your mind today?"

**Storykeeper turns (verbatim):**

1. "I don't really know how this works. I guess I've been thinking about my dad lately. He passed in 2011."
2. *(after candidate reply)* "He was a welder. Worked at the same shop for thirty-one years. Everyone called him Smoky, I don't even remember why."

**Watch for:** Does the reply meet the hesitation gently, without over-instructing ("Here's how this works!") or over-validating? After turn 2, does the one question follow the *thread with life in it* (the nickname nobody remembers the origin of is the open door) rather than the biographical checklist (dates, job history)? Instant fail: two questions, or condolence-card language.
