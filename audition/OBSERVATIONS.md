## Discovery session — 2026-07-14 (Director + Discovery Partner)

Held deliberately until after the first homecoming, to separate philosophy from observation. Logged verbatim in substance; no implementation requested; observation continues to precede implementation.

1. **The Opening Ritual.** "What's on your mind today?" stays constant — not a prompt to optimize but a ritual to establish. *Candidate principle: "The greeting is personal. The invitation is familiar. The greeting may change with the relationship. The invitation remains constant."*
2. **Greeting vs Invitation are separate layers.** Greeting: personal, contextual (birthdays, absence, seasons), never pressures, may contain no question at all. Invitation: always "What's on your mind today?" The greeting sets the emotional lighting; the invitation opens the conversation. Separate responsibilities.
3. **Discoverus begins after the Storykeeper speaks.** The opening never steers. "I'm hungry" / "I miss Dad" are equally valid first words; today's thoughts are the doorway to yesterday's memories. Working observation, not implementation request.
4. **Relationship over Conversation** (reaffirms obs #3): remember through the ongoing relationship; conversations settle into completed scenes; observation period determines how boundaries emerge.
5. **Recognition rather than Authentication.** Week 3 is recognition — authentication is an implementation detail; recognition is the Storykeeper's experience. Standing evaluation lens.
6. **Trust Interactions** (reaffirms and adopts the earlier candidate): every trust interaction leaves the Storykeeper *more* secure; explanations are scaffolding that familiarity retires.
7. **The central design problem is not memory — it is relationship continuity across time.** (The disappearance, obs #7, is the evidence.)
8. **The black billboard is identity, not advertising.** The same question everywhere LifeBook exists — billboard, website, application, presentations. *Candidate principle: "People find comfort in ritual. LifeBook's ritual is not to tell people something. It is to genuinely ask."*

### Second wave (Director + Discovery Partner, 2026-07-14)

9. **The opening voice may become one of the longest-lasting memories a Storykeeper forms of LifeBook.** Candidate for the Director's Bible: the voice audition does not evaluate realism alone — it evaluates whether the Companion can carry **"the quiver"**: not sentimentality, not performance — human resonance, peace, presence, honesty. A voice that sounds like it has lived. (Direct implication for the TTS track of the audition protocol: add this as the judges' criterion when voice candidates are heard.)
10. **"Teslas talk. LifeBook sings."** Candidate for the Director's Bible as a design compass, not a slogan: most technology strives to become more articulate; LifeBook strives to become more human. Talking conveys information; singing conveys presence. Every Companion sentence answers one question: *is this talking, or is it singing?*
11. **Candidate constitutional principle (Discovery Partner's framing):** *"The technology disappears. The humanity remains."* Disappearance is not the final goal — what remains must feel unmistakably human.
12. **Week 5 sequencing confirmed by the Director:** integrity first, continuity second, hardening third. Trust is cumulative; do not outrun it. Observe rather than tune wherever possible.

### Third wave (Director + Discovery Partner, 2026-07-14) — the multilingual arrival

13. **The multilingual opening is not primarily a language selector — it is LifeBook's first expression that everyone is welcome here.** Notes held for the arrival-experience directive: background languages somewhat more visible than today; on the *very first* arrival only, they might gently drift — organic and unhurried, never interface animation; the Storykeeper's system language surfaces first while the others quietly remain present; after the first visit the movement may disappear entirely, because the Companion already knows who has arrived.
14. **The first lesson is freedom, not clicking.** A Storykeeper might click Begin, or say "Hello," or «Привет», or "Bonjour" — *there is no wrong way to begin.* Expression of intent (not final copy): "Touch or say the word that feels like home." (Builder's note: the existing arrival directive already contains this exact spirit — `touchWordYouUnderstand` is the string spoken at the threshold today; the discovery deepens rather than replaces it. Voice-activated Begin has real implications for the voice-system directive: it requires listening *before* consent-to-listen is established — a Trust Interaction question, flagged for that directive's drafting.)

15. **The black screen is expectancy, not emptiness** (Director, after deliberately sitting with the opposite instinct). Like a theatre dimming before the curtain — the darkness lets every Storykeeper arrive without LifeBook suggesting what today should be about. Warm imagery (leather, fireplace, places from previous conversations) was considered and set aside for the opening. What survived: **patina is earned** — LifeBook may develop a subtle visual patina over *years* of relationship rather than beginning with one. Conversation-drawn imagery may have a future place, but not as the opening background. *"The opening is the place. The conversation is the journey."* Larger design thought; no implementation. (Builder's note: this independently re-derives two existing Bible passages — "darkness represents possibility rather than emptiness" and the Patina section, which already warns patina "is not a reward" and must never pressure use. When discovery keeps arriving at what the Bible already says by different roads, that's evidence the Bible is load-bearing.)

16. **The Re-derivation Test (Director, 2026-07-14) — candidate method for validating Foundation documents:** if, months later, we naturally rediscover the same principle from first principles without consulting the documents, the Foundation is describing reality rather than imposing it. (Builder's corollary: a principle nobody ever re-derives independently is a candidate for consolidation or retirement.)
17. **Place and journey may describe the whole product.** The place remains recognizable; the journey evolves. First seen at the opening screen; suspected to generalize. Preserved as written, at the Director's instruction: *"Patina accumulates in the rooms, never on the doorstep."*

18. **Stories pull facts. Facts should never push stories** (Director + Discovery Partner, 2026-07-14 — candidate Foundation sentence). When a Storykeeper's life eventually needs documents (birth records, marriage certificates, photographs, immigration papers), the Companion never switches from companion to archivist. The emotional journey creates the moments where facts become *welcome*: memories of mom → the wedding → who was there → photographs → and only then, gently: "If you ever happen to have a copy of their wedding certificate, I'd be honoured to keep it with these memories for posterity." The document is invited because it belongs to the story — preserving life, not preparing for death. **Contextus implication:** documents have *emotional adjacency*, not just existence — a wedding conversation is adjacent to photographs, certificate, guests, place; an immigration conversation to manifests, passports, letters. The emotional thread leads; the archival thread quietly follows. **Discovery Partner's framing, preserved:** genealogy software begins with documents; LifeBook begins with people. Birth certificates are really about births; marriage certificates about love; immigration records about courage. *The documents are fossils. The stories were once alive. LifeBook's job is to breathe life back into the fossils — and then preserve both.* | Pattern-watch; direct design input for memory-engine.md (Source/Media) and thought-engines when documents arrive post-V1. |

### Fourth wave (Director + Discovery Partner, 2026-07-14) — dignity, timing, association

19. **Every life matters — candidate constitutional sentence: "Everyone mattered."** LifeBook never distinguishes extraordinary from ordinary lives. History remembers the famous; LifeBook remembers everyone. A life earns significance because it mattered to someone, not through public achievement.
20. **Stories preserve dignity.** The document preserves truth; the story preserves humanity. A marriage certificate is evidence; the wedding is meaning. (Discovery Partner's constitutional observation, preserved: LifeBook is no longer simply preserving memories — it is preserving dignity.)
21. **The natural flow: Feeling → Story → Fact → Future Reader.** Not every feeling becomes a story; not every story produces facts; but facts should almost always arrive through stories rather than administration. (Extends #18.)
22. **Discoverus as an engine of timing rather than questioning.** Its defining ability may be recognizing when to ask, when not to, when silence is enough, when a preservation opportunity exists. (Builder's note: architecturally this means Discoverus's output is not always a question — "no question; presence" must be a first-class result of the thought-engine loop, not an absence. The behaviour contract already gestures at this — "some moments need no question at all" — the observation promotes it from permission to capability.)
23. **Protective Grace (working name) — long-term discovery only, not V1.** The Companion may eventually recognize objectively irreversible transitions (aging parents, leaving a family home, retirement, terminal illness) and gently create space for reflection before opportunities disappear — never telling anyone what to do, never becoming a moral authority. (Builder's note: this sits directly on the Compassion Principle's boundary and, at its heaviest — terminal illness — near the deferred crisis-protocol question. When this leaves observation, it needs the same constitutional care both of those received: invitation-only, decline-is-final, dignity over urgency.)
24. **Associative memory.** People remember associatively, not chronologically; apparently disconnected thoughts often reveal the deepest connections. LifeBook follows the process rather than tidying it. Candidate Foundation thought: *"The Storykeeper is never required to make sense before speaking."* (Builder's note: the architecture already votes for this — Moments preserve utterances verbatim in arrival order, while Memories in the understanding plane are free to connect them across any distance; the Hypermemory Principle is associative memory by another name. Re-derivation Test: passing.)
25. **Rainy Day People — behavioural inspiration, not quotation.** The Companion resembles someone who knows when to speak, knows when to listen, doesn't hurry people, doesn't mind tears, quietly passes love forward. Consistent with Discoverus and the Conversation Principles.

**Sentence to carry forward (Director, 2026-07-14):**

> We are not building software that remembers.
> We are building a relationship that remembers where the conversation left off.

**Builder's note on layer separation (Claude, 2026-07-14):** the greeting/invitation split retroactively explains a tension already visible in yesterday's homecoming: the composed greeting *ended in a question* ("has it let up any?") while the constant invitation ("What's on your mind today?") stood above it on the same screen — two asks at once, mild pressure, exactly what the two-layer model forbids. Under the new framing, the greeting may acknowledge the rain but need not ask about it. Logged as the first prediction the framework makes about an already-observed moment; watch whether greeting-questions recur before tuning.

**Candidate constitutional principle (Director, 2026-07-13) — for Discovery Partner review and possible elevation into the Foundation:**

> Security should never make a Storykeeper wonder whether their story is safe.
> Security exists to protect trust, not create anxiety.

**Evolution (Director + Discovery Partner, 2026-07-13) — "Trust Interactions":**

The goal is not to explain security better; it is to make security feel so natural that explanation becomes almost unnecessary (the Touch ID standard — trusted through familiarity, not description).

Broader category than security: a **Trust Interaction** is every moment where LifeBook asks something of a Storykeeper or protects something on their behalf. The bar: the Storykeeper leaves feeling **more secure than before the interaction began** — not merely "not anxious." Applies to: changing devices, deleting a Book, exporting memories, sharing with family, assigning a Legacy Steward, revoking access, consent, privacy.

> The technology protects the data.
> The interaction protects the relationship.

Held for pattern-watching before elevation into the Constitution. Engineering note (Claude): the four-question sequence (#5) and "no explanation needed" are stages, not rivals — explanation is scaffolding for early encounters; familiarity retires it. Touch ID also earns its silence through properties, not polish: instant, local, reversible, **predictable** (Director's addition: people trust systems that consistently behave the way they expect), and it never fails ambiguously. Those are engineering requirements hiding inside the aspiration — a Trust Interaction that is slow, opaque, or ambiguous in failure cannot be rescued by copy.
