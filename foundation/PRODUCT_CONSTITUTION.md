# 📜 LifeBook Product Constitution

> "Technology should preserve humanity, not replace it."

---

# Purpose

LifeBook exists to preserve identity, strengthen relationships, and protect the dignity of every person's story.

It is not another social network.

It is not another productivity application.

It is a living personal archive designed to help people remember, reflect, connect, and leave something meaningful behind.

---

# Mission

LifeBook helps people capture life as it happens—in words, voice, photographs, documents, memories, conversations, and relationships—and transforms those fragments into a coherent personal history that can be shared across generations.

Our mission is not to increase screen time.

Our mission is to increase understanding.

---

# Vision

We believe every human life deserves to be remembered.

LifeBook should become the trusted companion that quietly preserves a person's experiences from adolescence to old age, becoming more valuable every year instead of becoming obsolete.

The ultimate measure of success is not daily active users.

It is whether grandchildren can understand who someone truly was.

---

# Core Principles

## Humanity First

Technology exists to serve people.

People never exist to serve technology.

---

## Memory Over Metrics

We measure meaningful moments, not engagement.

Attention is never our product.

---

## Reflection Over Consumption

LifeBook encourages thinking.

It discourages endless scrolling.

Silence is a feature.

---

## Relationships Matter

A person's story is inseparable from the people who shaped it.

LifeBook should naturally strengthen family, friendship, mentorship, and community.

---

## AI as Companion

Artificial intelligence should assist.

It should never replace the user's voice.

AI organizes, translates, summarizes, remembers, and gently suggests.

The story always belongs to the person.

---

# What LifeBook Is

LifeBook is:

- a personal memory system
- a multilingual life journal
- a relationship archive
- a private family history
- a thinking partner
- a legacy platform
- a calm place to reflect

---

# What LifeBook Is NOT

LifeBook is not:

- social media
- another chat application
- another task manager
- another note-taking app
- another CRM
- another cloud drive
- an advertising platform
- an engagement engine

---

# Product Values

Every feature should increase one or more of the following:

- clarity
- memory
- connection
- empathy
- reflection
- understanding
- legacy

If it does not, it probably does not belong.

---

# Non-Negotiable UX Principles

The interface should feel like literature rather than software.

Users should feel calm.

Nothing should shout.

Nothing should compete for attention.

Beauty comes from restraint.

Complexity belongs behind the scenes.

---

# Language Philosophy

LifeBook belongs to everyone.

Every major capability should work across languages.

Translation should feel natural rather than mechanical.

People should be able to think, speak, write, and search in the language most natural to them.

Languages are first-class citizens, not optional features.

---

# Voice Philosophy

Voice is a primary input method.

Writing is equally respected.

Users may move seamlessly between speaking and typing without changing workflows.

The transcript always remains editable.

Speech should feel conversational rather than transactional.

---

# Privacy Principles

Users own their stories.

Users control sharing.

Privacy is the default.

Personal memories are never sold.

Advertising will never influence product decisions.

Trust is more valuable than growth.

---

# Accessibility

LifeBook should remain usable regardless of age, ability, language, or technical experience.

Accessibility is not a feature.

It is part of good design.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

Prefer simplicity over cleverness.

Prefer readability over brevity.

Prefer maintainability over novelty.

Prefer timeless solutions over fashionable ones.

Build foundations before features.

---

# Things We Will Never Do

We will never:

- monetize attention
- optimize for addiction
- manipulate emotions for engagement
- interrupt reflection with unnecessary notifications
- sell personal memories
- hide important information behind dark patterns
- force people into a single language
- replace authentic human expression with AI-generated personality

---

# Definition of Success

LifeBook succeeds when:

- families become closer
- memories become easier to preserve
- stories survive generations
- users feel understood
- technology fades into the background

If people forget they are using software and simply feel like they are preserving their lives, we have succeeded.
