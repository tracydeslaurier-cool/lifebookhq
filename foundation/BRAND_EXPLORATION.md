# Brand Exploration

> "A name should not merely identify the product. It should become synonymous with the feeling people experience when using it."

---

# Current Working Name

**LifeBook**

LifeBook has served as the working title since the earliest conception of the project.

It communicates:

- life
- legacy
- preservation
- personal history

However, it is already associated with multiple existing products and trademarks.

It remains our internal working name while we continue to discover the true identity of the product.

No final branding decision will be made until the product itself has fully emerged.

---

# What We Are Naming

We are not naming:

- software
- an AI assistant
- a journal
- a memoir application
- a genealogy platform

We are naming a trusted companion that helps people rediscover, preserve, and eventually share the memories that become the story of their life.

The name must be large enough to grow with that vision.

---

# What the Brand Must Evoke

The brand should suggest one or more of the following:

- trust
- warmth
- curiosity
- memory
- family
- legacy
- belonging
- humanity
- discovery
- companionship
- continuity
- hope

It should never suggest:

- surveillance
- productivity
- social media
- corporate software
- artificial intelligence
- automation
- clinical psychology
- self-help

---

# Our Guiding Principle

People should remember how LifeBook made them feel.

Not what technology powered it.

The same should ultimately become true of the brand itself.

---

# Naming Principles

The ideal name should be:

- timeless
- emotionally resonant
- easy to pronounce
- memorable
- globally usable
- culturally respectful
- available for trademark protection
- available as a primary internet domain if possible

The ideal name should not depend on current technology or trends.

---

# What Makes Us Different

Our competitive advantage is not AI.

It is not voice.

It is not memory storage.

It is our philosophy.

LifeBook believes:

- memories unlock stories
- people deserve to feel heard
- stories are discovered rather than extracted
- ordinary lives are extraordinary
- trust comes before technology

Any future brand must be able to carry these ideas.

---

# The Test

Before accepting any future name, ask:

Does this sound like a company that people could trust with the most precious memories of their family?

If the answer is anything less than an immediate yes...

it is not the right name.

---

# Timing

The product is still revealing its identity.

The name should emerge from the product rather than being imposed upon it.

Until that day arrives, "LifeBook" remains our working title.

We will know the right name when it feels inevitable.

# The Apple Principle

The greatest brands are rarely literal.

Apple is not about fruit.

Amazon is not about rivers.

Adobe is not about clay.

Pixar is not about animation.

The strongest brands acquire meaning through the experiences they create.

We should therefore resist choosing a name that merely describes what the product does.

Instead, we should seek a name that people will one day associate with trust, memory, family, and humanity.
