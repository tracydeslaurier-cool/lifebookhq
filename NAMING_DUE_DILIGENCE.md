# Naming Due Diligence — "LifeBook"

Status: PRELIMINARY RESEARCH — prepared by Claude, 2026-07-16, from public web sources.
**This is not legal advice or a trademark clearance opinion.** Formal clearance (CIPO + USPTO knockout search and availability opinion, Nice classes 9/41/42/45) requires a trademark lawyer before any permanent commitment.

---

## 1. lifebook.com — unavailable in practice

Owned by **Fujitsu Technology Solutions GmbH** since **1997**; registrar-locked, renewed April 2026, currently serving an empty page — a dormant defensive asset supporting their LIFEBOOK laptop brand. Not parked, not listed, no sale history. Acquisition path: stealth buyer-broker approach to a corporation with a live trademark and no pressure to sell — realistic outcomes are **"no," or strong six figures** — and success would still not resolve the trademark exposure, which stays with Fujitsu regardless of who owns the domain.

## 2. The trademark field — crowded precisely where LifeBook lives

The concern is not Fujitsu's laptops (Class 9 hardware, registered and renewed to ~2031, still shipping new LIFEBOOK models in 2026). The concern is **four parties already in or adjacent to our exact category**:

| Who | What | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Jon Butcher / Mindvalley — "LIFEBOOK"** | **Registered** USPTO Class 9 *software* mark (Reg. 5392138, 2018): personal development, "life vision" | A registered software mark on the exact word; actively sold (mindvalley.com/lifebook, mylifebook.com) |
| **Lifebook Memoirs Ltd (UK)** | Active global memoir/private-autobiography service; **three 2023 USPTO applications** covering preserving personal and family histories | Our service space, nearly verbatim |
| **joinlifebook.com** | Live digital-legacy SaaS: "leave messages, videos and memories for loved ones after you pass away," subscription-priced | A near-identical product concept already trading as "Lifebook" |
| **"LIFEBOOK AI"** | USPTO application filed **Aug 2025**, Class 42 SaaS: AI transcription, summarization, **archiving, indexing and retrieval** | An AI mark, one year old, squarely over our mechanics |

Plus assorted others (Wells Fargo legacy filing for personal-document software, health-records and estate-planning LifeBooks, an art course, an evangelical publishing program). Justia lists **32 LIFEBOOK-family marks**. Canadian register (CIPO) could not be queried directly — Fujitsu asserts rights in "the United States and other countries," and a Canadian registration is likely; **CIPO confirmation is the single most important open item for a Canadian company and needs the lawyer**.

## 4. Stewardship assessment

The Foundation called this in advance: Brand Exploration designates LifeBook a **working title**, notes the trademark crowding, and rules that "no final branding decision will be made until the product itself has fully emerged." The research converts that caution into fact:

1. **"LifeBook" carries real legal risk as a public brand in exactly our category.** A registered software mark (Butcher), a memoir service with fresh filings, a trading digital-legacy SaaS, and a pending AI-archiving mark form a fence around the name. Public launch under it invites opposition, coexistence negotiations, or a forced rename at the worst possible time — after Storykeepers have formed an attachment.
2. **Spending meaningfully on LifeBook domains now would be paying for risk.** $6–7k for .app/.io buys deeper commitment to a name we may be legally unable to keep.
3. **The interim need (a doorway-email domain) is real but small — and should stay cheap and neutral.** lifebookhq.com (if we own it — confirm) serves staging honestly: `hello@lifebookhq.com` reads warmly, costs nothing new, and creates no false permanence. A staging sender domain is not the brand.
4. **The permanent name is the Discovery Partner's process, run when the Bible says to run it** ("the name should emerge from the product rather than being imposed upon it") — followed by lawyer-run clearance *before* any public use, any expensive domain, or any attachment formed. Notably, observation #43 ("LifeBook is the relationship that exists between them") may be the first real input to that process: the thing being named is a relationship, and the right name may not contain "book" or "life" at all.

## 5. Recommendation

- **Interim (now):** confirm/secure **lifebookhq.com** (or an equivalently cheap neutral domain); sender `hello@lifebookhq.com`; proceed with staging. Explicitly interim; no six-figure conversations, no aftermarket purchases.
- **Permanent (later, before public launch):** naming discovery with the Discovery Partner → shortlist → **trademark lawyer clearance in Canada and the US** → then, and only then, domain acquisition. Budget attention for the lawyer before the domain.
- **Log for the record:** the Re-derivation Test applies to names too — if, months from now, the product keeps telling us its name is LifeBook despite the fence, that is information; but we would still need the lawyer to tell us whether we may keep it.
